About

I am a computational astrophysicist studying dust in supernova remnants and in the interstellar medium. As a postdoc in the Department of Physics & Astronomy at Ghent University, I investigate the transport, destruction and growth of dust in a moving gas.

In the past, I also worked on dust in circumstellar discs and the optical properties of non-spherical dust grains.

My Research

The focus of my research is the evolution of dust in supernova shocks. I developed the code Paperboats to study dust transport and destruction by sputtering, vaporization, or fragmentation. I also examine dust growth via coagulation, gas accretion or ion trapping.

My work involves both simulations and observations, including projects with JWST and VLT. I also study exozodiacal dust in main-sequence star systems.
