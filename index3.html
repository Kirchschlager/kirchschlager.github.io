About Me

I am a computational astrophysicist studying dust in supernova remnants and in the interstellar medium. My research at Ghent University focuses on dust transport, destruction, and growth in moving gases.

I also investigate dust in circumstellar discs and the optical properties of non-spherical dust grains using both simulations and observations (e.g., JWST, VLT).

Research Focus

Using my code Paperboats, I explore dust processing in supernova remnants—particularly dust sputtering, fragmentation, and ejection from the ejecta to the ISM.

We simulate supernovae in turbulent, magnetized ISM environments, finding that inhomogeneities and magnetic fields reduce dust destruction.

I study exozodiacal dust, grain shape effects, and light polarization in protoplanetary and debris discs using radiative transfer modeling and IR observations.
