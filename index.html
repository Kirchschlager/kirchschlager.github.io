My research

The focus of my current research is the investigation of dust destruction processes in shocks impacting on supernova remnants. Furthermore, I have been worked both on observational and theoretical studies in the field of evolution of protoplanetary disks using near-infrared observations and 3D radiative transfer simulations. I concentrated on the influence of dust properties like grain porosity on the appearance of protoplanetary and debris disks as well as on modelling of debris disks. Beyond that, I am further interested in the origin and the nature of hot exozodiacal dust around main-sequence stars, and on the determination and investigation of dust grain growth in laboratory plasmas.
