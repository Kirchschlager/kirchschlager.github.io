About

I'm an astrophysicist studying dust in circumstellar disks and supernova remnants. I'm in the group of Prof. Dr. Mike J. Barlow in the Department of Physics & Astronomy at University College London (UCL) since May 2017. Before moving to UCL I was at Kiel University in Germany in the group of Prof. Dr. Sebastian Wolf.

My research

The focus of my current research is the investigation of dust destruction processes in shocks impacting on supernova remnants. For this purpose, I have developed the dust-processing code Paperboats ( Kirchschlager et al. 2019b). My second current research field comprises the observation and modelling of circumstellar disks, in particular hot exozodiacal dust systems around main-sequence stars. Beyond that, I have worked in the last few years on both observational and theoretical projects in the field of protoplanetary disks using near-infrared observations and 3D radiative transfer simulations. I concentrated on the influence of dust properties like grain porosity on the appearance of protoplanetary and debris disks as well as on modelling of debris disks. I am further interested in dust grain growth experiments in laboratory plasmas.
